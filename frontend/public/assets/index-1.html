Informational Legal Articles

Educational articles on common legal matters in India. For informational purposes only — not legal advice.

Civil Law
Understanding Civil Litigation in India

Civil litigation refers to legal disputes between parties seeking monetary compensation or specific remedies. Governed by the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, a civil suit begins with filing a plaint, followed by summons, written statement, framing of issues, evidence, arguments, and judgment. Interim applications for injunctions or stays may be filed at various stages.

Consumer Law
How to File a Consumer Complaint in Telangana

Under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, complaints for claims up to ₹50 lakhs are filed before the District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission. Claims between ₹50 lakhs and ₹2 crore go to the State Commission. A written complaint with supporting documents and proof of purchase is required. Legal representation is not mandatory but may assist.

NI Act
What Happens in a Cheque Bounce Case

A dishonoured cheque attracts liability under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881. A demand notice must be sent within 30 days of the cheque return memo. If payment isn't made within 15 days, a complaint may be filed before the Magistrate. Conviction may result in imprisonment up to two years or a fine up to twice the cheque amount.

Property Law
Property Disputes & Title Verification in Telangana

Property disputes commonly arise from unclear title chains, fraudulent transfers, and encroachments. Title verification involves examining sale deeds, encumbrance certificates, patta records, and mutation entries. RERA Telangana regulates disputes relating to registered real estate projects. Proceedings are filed before Civil Courts or the High Court depending on valuation.

Family Law
Overview of Matrimonial Proceedings in India

Matrimonial disputes are governed by personal laws including the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 and Special Marriage Act, 1954. Family Courts under the Family Courts Act, 1984 handle divorce, maintenance, and custody proceedings. Courts must explore reconciliation before proceeding with divorce. Child custody is decided on the basis of the best interests of the child.

Constitutional Law
When Can You File a Writ Petition in the High Court?

Writ petitions are filed before the High Court under Article 226 of the Constitution to enforce fundamental rights or challenge unlawful actions of state authorities. The five writs — habeas corpus, mandamus, prohibition, certiorari, and quo warranto — apply in specific situations. The High Court of Telangana exercises jurisdiction over all matters arising within Telangana.
